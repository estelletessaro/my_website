Task 3: Brexit vote analysis

The last scatterplot we created shows a great correlation between people who were born in the UK, and voted to leave the European Union. There are several common explanations for this phenomenon. As mentionned above, one explanation could be a fear of immigration, and thus an aversion to the EU’s border policy. Looking into the data more deeply, it is obvious that the regions with the highest counts of leave votes were in rural areas, where immigrant communities are limited, compared to the larger cities. It would make sense that a lack of these communities would foster a “fear of the unknown”.

Task 4: Animal rescue incidents attended by the London Fire Brigade

Which of these four graphs do you think best communicates the variability of the incident_notional_cost values? Also, can you please tell some sort of story (which animals are more expensive to rescue than others, the spread of values) and speculate about the differences in the patterns.

From the plots we have created, it seem that animals such as cows, ferrets, and rabbits have a great variability in notional costs. However, more commonly rescued animals such as cats and birds have similar respective notional costs, for the most part. This could be attributed to the fact that the latter are rescued more often, and thus have very generalized rescue procedures, whereas cows and rabbits are rescured more rarely, and thus are treated on a case basis (which can increase the cost quickly). I would say that the cow, ferret, rabbit and snake plots best communicate the variability of the incident_notional_cost values.
